Threat Vectors

Fraud Entry Points

Phishing & AiTM Adversary-in-the-Middle session theft

Critical · Session Hijack

Modern phishing has outgrown the clumsy email that begs for your password. Adversary-in-the-Middle (AiTM) kits deploy a reverse proxy that sits invisibly between you and the genuine login portal. The page you see is the real one — relayed live — so every field, logo, and security prompt looks perfectly authentic.

  • Live relay: your username, password, and MFA code pass straight through to the legitimate server the instant you type them.
  • The cookie is the prize: once you clear MFA, the server issues a session token — the proxy silently copies it before it ever reaches your browser.
  • Push & SMS are defeated: because you genuinely approved the login, push notifications and one-time SMS codes offer zero protection.
  • Replay: the stolen cookie is imported into the attacker's browser, granting a fully authenticated session without ever needing your password again.

AI Voice Cloning & Deepfakes Synthetic identity from 3 seconds of audio

Critical · Impersonation

Generative audio models no longer need a recording studio. A three-second clip — scraped from an Instagram reel, a voicemail greeting, a podcast cameo, or a TikTok — is enough to synthesize a convincing clone of your voice, reproducing your accent, pace, and emotional inflection.

  • Voiceprint bypass: banks and call centers that use "my voice is my password" biometrics can be fooled by a synthetic sample played down the line.
  • Family emergency scams: cloned voices phone relatives feigning a car crash, arrest, or kidnapping to extract urgent wires and gift cards.
  • Executive impersonation: deepfaked audio and video of a CFO or manager authorize fraudulent payments and credential resets (CEO fraud).
  • Real-time conversion: live voice-changing now lets a scammer hold an interactive two-way conversation in a stolen voice.

SIM Swapping & Quishing Number takeover & weaponized QR codes

High · Account Takeover

Two attacks converge on the device you trust most. One steals your phone number; the other weaponizes the camera you use to scan everyday codes.

  • SIM swap: attackers pretext or bribe carrier staff — armed with breached personal data or a forged ID — to port your number onto a SIM they control.
  • Number equals identity: the moment the port completes, every SMS one-time code, call-based reset, and recovery text is delivered to the attacker.
  • Quishing (QR phishing): malicious QR codes pasted over parking meters, mailed on fake notices, or embedded in PDFs slip past email scanners because they are images, not links.
  • Mobile blind spot: a phone's small screen hides the true destination URL, so a hijacked session or credential-harvest page is far harder to spot.

The Security Stack

Tactical Defensive Manual

Identity & Access Control Phishing-resistant authentication

Layer 01 · Credentials
  1. 1

    Deploy a dedicated password manager

    Install a standalone vault and migrate every credential out of your browser's built-in store — those are tied to a single, often weakly-protected profile. Generate unique 16+ character random passwords for each account, then write your master password and emergency recovery sentence onto paper and seal it in a physical safe. Never store the master key in a digital note.

  2. 2

    Transition master accounts to Passkeys

    Convert your highest-value identity providers — Google, Apple, Microsoft — to Passkeys, unlocked by local biometrics (Face ID, Touch ID, Windows Hello). Because the private key never leaves your device and is cryptographically bound to the real domain, AiTM proxies and cloned pages have nothing to steal.

  3. 3

    Register a primary + backup hardware key

    Enroll a physical FIDO2 / WebAuthn security key as your strongest factor, then enroll a second key as backup and lock it in a separate location. The key verifies the legitimate domain in silicon — immune to real-time phishing relays — and a spare means losing one device never locks you out.

Communication Infrastructure Lock the carrier & the inbound channel

Layer 02 · Telecom
  1. 1

    Lock your number at the carrier

    Phone your mobile provider and explicitly request a Port-Out Lock / Number Lock and a SIM PIN. Add a separate verbal passphrase or account PIN that must be spoken before any change is processed. This single step is the most effective defense against SIM swapping.

  2. 2

    Filter the inbound channel

    Enable Silence Unknown Callers so unrecognized numbers route straight to voicemail — iOS: Settings → Apps → Phone · Android: Phone → Settings → Caller ID & spam. Layer a dedicated call-screening service on top to block known robocall and spoofing campaigns, and register on the national do-not-call list to cut baseline volume.

Asset & Vault Safeguards Freeze credit & encrypt the cloud

Layer 03 · Assets
  1. 1

    Freeze all three credit bureaus

    Place a security freeze with each of the three major bureaus individually — Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. A freeze blocks new lines of credit from being opened in your name, is free to set and lift, and is the cornerstone of identity protection. Use a monitoring service to watch all three from one dashboard.

  2. 2

    Encrypt your cloud nodes (E2EE)

    Turn on end-to-end encryption for your cloud backups and storage. On Apple devices, enable Advanced Data Protection — Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Advanced Data Protection — to extend E2EE across iCloud Backup, Photos, and Notes so not even the provider can read them. If your identity is already compromised, file an official recovery plan immediately.
