What to do the moment someone unexpected contacts you

Do they claim to be someone you know?

NO

Golden rule: Legitimate banks, the IRS, SSA, Apple, Google, and Microsoft will never ask for your password, PIN, gift card codes, wire transfers, or remote access over the phone.

Red Flags — Hang Up Immediately

  • Threatening arrest, account closure, or legal action unless you act right now
  • Asking for gift card numbers, wire transfer, or crypto to "resolve" an issue
  • Requesting remote access to your computer or phone ("tech support")
  • Claiming to know your partial SSN and asking you to "confirm" the rest
  • Telling you not to tell your family or bank about the call
  • Caller ID looks legitimate — it's easily spoofed; do not trust it
  • Sending a link via text and demanding you click it immediately
  • Claiming to be Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, IRS, or Social Security Administration

Safe Signals — Probably Legitimate

  • They don't ask for passwords, PINs, or full SSN over the phone
  • They encourage you to call back on a number you find yourself
  • No urgency — they say you can take your time or call at your convenience
  • Communication arrives via secure in-app message or official email domain
  • They never ask you to move money to "protect" it

Common Scam Scenarios

  • Grandparent scam: AI-cloned voice of a family member — "in trouble, send money now"
  • Bank fraud alert: "Suspicious charges — verify your card number to confirm it's you"
  • IRS / SSA: Government agencies never call demanding immediate payment
  • Utility shutoff: "Pay with gift cards or your power is cut today"
  • Prize / lottery: You won! Just pay the processing fee first
  • Quishing: QR code in a text, email, or pasted over a sign — destination hidden
The Six Security Pillars
Every area you must lock down — key actions per layer
Passwords & Identity
Layer 1
  • Use a dedicated password manager — not your browser
  • Generate a unique 16+ char random password per account
  • Write master password on paper and store in a physical safe
  • Enable Passkeys on Google / Apple ID for phishing resistance
  • Register a FIDO2 hardware key (+ backup key stored separately)
Banking & Finance
Layer 2
  • Freeze credit at Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion — free
  • Enable real-time transaction alerts for every purchase
  • Use virtual card numbers for online shopping
  • Set low daily wire / Zelle transfer limits
  • Enable the strongest available MFA on every financial account
Communications
Layer 3
  • Call carrier: add port-out lock, SIM PIN, and verbal passphrase
  • Enable "Silence Unknown Callers" in iOS / Android settings
  • Use a call-blocking app (RoboKiller, Hiya, Nomorobo)
  • Never click links in unexpected texts — go directly to the app
  • Use E2EE messaging (Signal) for sensitive conversations
Devices & Apps
Layer 4
  • Auto-updates enabled for OS and all apps at all times
  • Strong biometric + 6-digit PIN screen lock, auto-lock ≤ 1 min
  • Install apps from official stores only; delete unused apps
  • Review app permissions quarterly — revoke camera/mic/location if unused
  • Use a VPN on any public Wi-Fi network
Data & Backups
Layer 5
  • Enable Advanced Data Protection on iPhone (E2EE iCloud)
  • Offline backup to an encrypted external drive monthly
  • 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite
  • Enable FileVault (Mac) or BitLocker (Windows) full-disk encryption
  • Cross-cut shred all physical mail with account / SSN info
Monitoring & Alerts
Layer 6
  • Check HaveIBeenPwned monthly; enable email breach notifications
  • Set up Google / Apple dark web email monitoring
  • Pull free annual credit report from each bureau via AnnualCreditReport.com
  • Set Google Alerts on your name + last 4 of SSN
  • Review all account activity weekly — dispute charges within 60 days
Bank & Financial Account Security
Eight steps to lock down your money
01
Freeze All Three Credit Bureaus
Place a free security freeze at Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion individually — all three must be done separately. Blocks new lines of credit from being opened. Unfreeze temporarily when you need new credit.
02
Enable Every Transaction Alert
Set SMS + email alerts for every transaction, not just large ones. A fraudster often tests with a $0.99 charge before a large one. Bank App → Settings → Alerts → All Transactions
03
Use Authenticator App MFA (Not SMS)
SMS one-time codes are defeated by SIM swapping. Where your bank supports it, switch to an authenticator app (Authy, Google Authenticator) or a FIDO2 hardware key — the strongest available option.
04
Set Low Daily Transfer Limits
Reduce your Zelle, wire transfer, and bill pay limits to your realistic daily maximum. An attacker who gets in can only move that amount. Raise limits in-app or by calling the bank for large one-off transfers.
05
Use Virtual Card Numbers Online
Privacy.com (US) generates single-use or merchant-locked virtual card numbers. Your real card number never touches an external site. Many banks also offer this natively under "Manage Card" settings.
06
Never Trust an Inbound "Bank" Call
Hang up. Look at the back of your card or the bank's official website and call that number. Banks never ask for full card numbers, PINs, or OTP codes over an inbound call — that is always fraud.
07
Dedicate a Separate Email for Finance
Create an email address used only for bank and financial accounts — never for newsletters, social media, or purchases. Reduces spearphishing risk significantly since the address is not publicly linked to your identity.
08
Review Statements Weekly
US law (Regulation E / FDIC) protects you if you dispute unauthorized charges within 60 days. Weekly reviews take under 2 minutes in your bank's app. Flag anything unrecognised — even tiny amounts.

How to Enable 2FA on Your Most Important Accounts

Google Account
myaccount.google.com → Security → 2-Step Verification → Get started
Apple ID
Settings → [Your Name] → Sign-In & Security → Two-Factor Authentication
Microsoft Account
account.microsoft.com → Security → Advanced security options → 2-step verification
Facebook / Instagram
Settings → Password and Security → Two-factor authentication → Authentication App
Your Bank
Look for "Security" or "Login" in your mobile banking app. Call support if the 2FA option isn't visible — ask for "authenticator app 2FA".
Coinbase / Crypto
Profile → Security → 2-Step Verification → Select Authenticator App — never SMS for crypto accounts.
